# Frictionshift: Technical Definition

    ## Formal Definition
    The strategic relocation of transaction costs and barriers across different stages of the customer journey to maximize extraction while maintaining the perception of low friction. Platforms create "free" entry points but impose significant costs at exit or intermediate stages (e.g., free signup with expensive cancellation fees, or low advertised prices with hidden final costs).

    ## Conceptual Background
    Frictionshift is used here as an operational concept rather than a rhetorical label. It addresses strategic relocation of transaction frictions across user journey stages. The mechanism focus is low-friction entry combined with deferred costs and constraints at conversion, usage, or exit points, which can be represented with explicit channels: entry_friction_relief, mid_journey_friction, exit_friction, hidden_fee_intensity, switching_penalty, cancellation_cost, transparency_level. This repository couples concept definition with executable implementation so assumptions remain inspectable. In practice, the framework supports comparative statics, scenario simulation, and policy diagnostics. Typical applications include platform design audits, consumer welfare analysis, and dark-pattern enforcement support. Because the index logic is transparent, researchers can vary weights and test robustness across model specifications.
